Ghibli Studio

Studio Ghibli Inc.[a] is a Japanese animation film studio headquartered in Koganei, Tokyo.[1] It is best known for its animated feature films, and has also produced several short subjects, television commercials, and one television film. Its mascot and most recognizable symbol is a character named Totoro, a giant catlike spirit from the 1988 anime film My Neighbor Totoro. Among the studio's highest-grossing films are Spirited Away (2001), Howl's Moving Castle (2004) and Ponyo (2008).

Ghibli Studio's Work

Castle in the Sky :

Laputa: Castle in the Sky, known as Tenkū no Shiro: Laputa[a] in Japan and Castle in the Sky in North America, is a 1986 Japanese animated fantasy adventure film written and directed by Hayao Miyazaki.

Ponyo :

Ponyo (Japanese: 崖の上のポニョ, Hepburn: Gake no Ue no Ponyo, 'Ponyo on the Cliff by the Sea')[note 1] is a 2008 Japanese animated fantasy film written and directed by Hayao Miyazaki, animated by Studio Ghibli for the Nippon Television Network, Dentsu, Hakuhodo DY Media Partners, Buena Vista Home Entertainment, Mitsubishi, and distributed by Toho.
